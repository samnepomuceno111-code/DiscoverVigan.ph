Landmarks

Historical Landmarks

Bantay Well Tower

The Bantay Well Tower is a Spanish-period watchtower situated on a hill overlooking Vigan and Bantay. Visitors can climb to the top to enjoy panoramic views of the city, nearby mountains, and lush landscapes. The tower also played a role in guarding the town during early colonial times.

Vigan Cathedral

Built in the 1600s, the Vigan Cathedral showcases the Earthquake Baroque architectural style. It sits at the city center, facing Plaza Burgos. The church is known for its majestic façade, tall bell tower, and serene interior. As one of Vigan’s most iconic structures, the cathedral continues to serve as a major religious and historical landmark.

Calle Crisologo

Calle Crisologo is the heart of Vigan’s heritage district. This beautifully preserved cobblestone street is lined with Spanish-era ancestral homes, antique shops, and cafés. Walking here—especially at night when warm lights illuminate the houses—feels like stepping back into the 18th century. Kalesa (horse-drawn carriage) rides enhance the old-world experience.

Bantay Church

Located beside the bell tower, Bantay Church is known for its rustic red-brick façade and long-standing religious significance. The church holds stories of faith and resilience, making it a beloved structure for both locals and tourists.

Syquia Mansion

Syquia Mansion is a grand ancestral house once home to the family of former Philippine President Elpidio Quirino. Inside, you’ll find antique furniture, vintage portraits, and architectural details that reflect the lifestyle of Vigan’s elite during the Spanish era. The mansion is one of the best examples of preserved bahay-na-bato design.

Padre Burgos House

The birthplace of Fr. José Burgos—one of the martyred priests known as Gomburza—this museum highlights Ilocano heritage and significant moments in Philippine history. The exhibits include traditional tools, colonial furniture, and historical artifacts that offer insight into life during the Spanish era.

Cultural Landmarks

Pagburnayan Jar Factory

The Pagburnayan is Vigan’s traditional pottery center. Skilled artisans use ancient techniques to mold clay into burnay jars, which were historically used for storing water, vinegar, and wine. Visitors can watch the process or try making pottery themselves. This site showcases Vigan’s long-standing craftsmanship.

Abel Iloco Weaving Centers

These weaving centers preserve the traditional art of abel weaving using manual wooden looms. Textiles produced here are known for their durability, patterns, and cultural significance. Visitors can watch the weaving process and buy handwoven products such as blankets, scarves, and table runners.

Plaza Salcedo

Plaza Salcedo is the central square of Vigan, surrounded by historic Spanish-era buildings and heritage structures. It serves as a hub for cultural events, festivals, and community gatherings, and is famous for its nighttime dancing fountain show, where colorful lights and water move in sync with music, creating a lively and picturesque atmosphere for visitors.
