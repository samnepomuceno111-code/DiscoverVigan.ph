Foods

Must try delicacies!

Vigan Empanada

A deep-fried pastry with a thin, crispy, pale crust made from rice flour. It is filled with a mixture of grated green papaya, Vigan longganisa (a garlicky and vinegary sausage), and a whole raw egg, which cooks inside the empanada. It is often eaten with sukang Iloko for dipping.

Bagnet

Crispy, deep-fried pork belly, originating from the Ilocos region of the Philippines. It is prepared by first boiling the pork belly and then deep-frying it, often twice, to achieve a crispy skin and tender meat. It is typically served with sauces, such as bagoong and Sukang Ilocos.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet from Vigan is a hearty vegetable stew from the Ilocos region, known for being flavored with fermented shrimp paste and featuring locally sourced ingredients like bagnet (crispy pork belly), tomatoes, eggplant, bitter melon, okra, squash, and long beans.

Royal Bibingka

Royal Bibingka is a thick, dense, mildly sweet sticky rice cake topped with melted cheese, made from glutinous rice flour, coconut milk, and eggs. It's chewy, soft when fresh, and a perfect hot merienda snack.

Vigan Longganisa

Vigan longganisa is a small, plump pork sausage, salty and garlicky with sour notes from vinegar, soy sauce, annatto, and spices. Air-dried in hog casings, it's fried in its fat for breakfast with rice and vinegar dip.

Poqui-Poqui

The dish is often described as an eggplant hash or a deconstructed tortang talong (eggplant omelette) with a creamy and smoky flavor profile. It's a healthy, budget-friendly, and filling meal option that is high in fiber and a good source of vitamins A and C

Dinengdeng

Dinengdeng, also known as inabraw, is a traditional, healthy Ilocano vegetable stew. It is characterized by its use of diverse, fresh, locally-sourced vegetables in a light broth flavored prominently with bagoong isda and typically topped with fried or grilled fish.

Sinanglaw

Sinanglaw is a traditional Filipino sour soup dish. It is a hearty beef (sometimes pork) and beef offal soup known for its sour taste, which typically comes from kamias or tamarind, and a hint of bitterness from bile.

Vigan Pipian

Vigan pipian is a unique Ilocano porridge made from toasted ground rice, chicken, and a strong, aromatic herb called pasotes. The dish is a variant of the Mexican pipian, with the Filipino version adapted to local ingredients, and is often served as a merienda or light meal.
