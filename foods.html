Foods

Must try delicacies!

Vigan Empanada

A deep-fried pastry with a thin, crispy, pale crust made from rice flour. It is filled with a mixture of grated green papaya, Vigan longganisa (a garlicky and vinegary sausage), and a whole raw egg, which cooks inside the empanada. It is often eaten with sukang Iloko (Ilocano vinegar) for dipping.

Bagnet

Crispy, deep-fried pork belly, originating from the Ilocos region of the Philippines. It is prepared by first boiling the pork belly and then deep-frying it, often twice, to achieve a crispy skin and tender meat. It is typically served with sauces, such as bagoong and Sukang Ilocos.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet from Vigan is a hearty vegetable stew from the Ilocos region, known for being flavored with fermented shrimp paste and featuring locally sourced ingredients like bagnet (crispy pork belly), tomatoes, eggplant, bitter melon, okra, squash, and long beans.
